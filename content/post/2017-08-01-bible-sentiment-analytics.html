Natural Language Processing

Natural Language Processing, usually shortened as NLP, is a branch of artificial intelligence that deals with the interaction between computers and humans using the natural language. The goal of NLP is to read, decipher, and understand human languages objectively using machine learning to derive meaning from communication.

Read between the lines

The rules that dictate the passing of information using natural languages are at time high-leveled and abstract such as someones use of a sarcastic remark. On the other hand, some of these rules can be trivial like using the character “s” to signify the plurality of items. To understand the human language comprehensively requires knowledge of words and how concepts are connected to deliver the intended message.

While humans can easily master a language, the ambiguity and imprecise characteristics of the natural languages are what make NLP difficult for machines to implement.

In Practice

Natural Language Processing is the driving force behind the following common applications:

  • Language translation applications such as Google Translate
  • Word Processors such as Microsoft Word and Grammarly that employ NLP to check grammatical accuracy of texts.
  • Interactive Voice Response (IVR) applications used in call centers to respond to certain users’ requests.
  • Personal assistant applications such as OK Google, Siri, Cortana, and Alexa.
